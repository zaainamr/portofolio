Overview

DSC Research is a comprehensive user research initiative conducted during my internship at Telkomsel. The primary goal was to analyze the existing Digital Smart Care (DSC) application and identify areas for improvement to enhance the operational efficiency of field technicians and agents.

This research relied heavily on qualitative methods, specifically in-depth interviews and direct observation. By engaging directly with users and observing their daily workflows, I was able to uncover deep-rooted pain points and inefficiencies that quantitative data alone could not reveal.

Research Goals

Understand User Needs

Gain deep empathy for users through interviews to understand their daily struggles and needs.

Analyze Workflow

Directly observe users working with the DSC system to identify process bottlenecks.

Identify Improvements

Synthesize findings to propose 36+ actionable capabilities for system enhancement.

Methodology

01

In-Depth Interviews

Conducted structured and semi-structured interviews with IPTV agents and managers to gather subjective feedback and personal experiences with the system.

02

Direct Observation

Performed shadowing sessions to watch users interact with the application in real-time, allowing for the discovery of unarticulated habits and workarounds.

03

Data Synthesis

Utilized techniques like Empathy Mapping and User Journey Mapping to organize qualitative data into coherent user stories and problem statements.

04

Insight Generation

Translated research findings into concrete feature requirements (Capabilities) to improve the Digital Smart Care system's overall effectiveness and user satisfaction.
